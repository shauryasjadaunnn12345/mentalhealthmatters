Understanding Anxiety Disorders

What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is more than worry—it's a real mental health condition that can interfere with your ability to live, work, and connect. Common forms include:

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  • Panic Disorder
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Specific Phobias

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent worry or fear without clear cause
  • Physical tension, restlessness, or irritability
  • Rapid heartbeat, sweating, shaking
  • Trouble concentrating or sleeping
  • Avoiding triggers like people or places

What You Can Do

Anxiety is treatable. Your first step could be one of our free assessments, or exploring therapy, medication, or lifestyle changes such as:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): Restructure negative thoughts into positive change
  • Medication: Short-term or long-term support depending on your symptoms
  • Self-care practices: Regular sleep, exercise, mindfulness, and journaling

Frequently Asked Questions

Are your mental health tests scientifically validated?

Yes, all our assessments are based on clinically validated scales used by mental health professionals worldwide. We adapt established screening tools for online use while maintaining their scientific accuracy.

Is my data private and secure?

Absolutely. We don't collect, store, or share any personal information. All assessments are completed anonymously, and no data is saved on our servers. Your privacy is our top priority.

Can these tests replace professional diagnosis?

Our tests are screening tools, not diagnostic instruments. They can help identify potential symptoms but cannot replace a professional evaluation. Always consult with a qualified healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment.

How often should I take these assessments?

It depends on your needs. Some people find it helpful to take assessments every few months to track changes, while others may use them when experiencing new or worsening symptoms. There's no set schedule—use them as you feel appropriate.
