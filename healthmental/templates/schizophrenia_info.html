What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a complex mental health disorder that affects a person's ability to distinguish reality from illusion. It may involve auditory or visual hallucinations, paranoid beliefs, and cognitive disruptions. Common types include paranoid, schizoaffective, and borderline schizophrenia.

Common Symptoms

  • Hearing voices or seeing things that aren't there (hallucinations)
  • False beliefs that persist despite evidence (delusions)
  • Confused thinking or incoherent speech
  • Social withdrawal and emotional numbness
  • Lack of motivation or interest in daily activities

Who Should Take This Test?

Our self-assessment is designed for adults, teens, and individuals who may be experiencing early signs of psychosis or schizophrenia-like symptoms. It can also help concerned parents screen for potential teenage schizophrenia.

Why Early Screening Is Vital

Identifying schizophrenia early can drastically improve outcomes. Early treatment through therapy and medication helps manage symptoms and enhance quality of life. This quiz serves as a first step toward mental health clarity.
